# Design System: Mr. Guy Mobile Detail

## Overview

This product should feel clean, fast, trustworthy, and mobile-first. It is a service-business funnel, not a boutique auto-detailing fantasy site. The user should understand the offer, trust the operator, and feel like booking is easier than procrastinating.

## Color Rules

- `primaryBlue` owns primary actions and visual momentum.
- `deepBlue` is for high-contrast headings and structural emphasis.
- Backgrounds should stay sky-tinted and clean, not dark, glossy, or "premium black."
- White surfaces are used for forms, cards, and overlays.

## Typography Rules

- Use strong, readable sans-serif typography with confident weight.
- Headlines should be short, direct, and offer-led.
- Supporting copy should stay simple and practical.

## Layout Principles

- Mobile is the default. Primary CTA and package selection must stay easy to reach.
- Show the offer fast: what it is, what it costs, and how quickly the customer can act.
- Use clear packages, obvious status banners, and low-friction proof.
- The site may use motion and gradient backgrounds, but the booking path must stay plain and legible.

## Elevation and Shape

- Cards are soft and clean with moderate radius.
- Sections can use layered sky gradients, but controls should remain grounded and crisp.
- Hover motion is acceptable on desktop as long as it does not slow scanning or interaction.

## Components

### Buttons

- Primary CTA: solid sky blue, white text, obvious hover darkening.
- Secondary CTA: white or pale surface with clear border and readable text.

### Package Menu

- This is the heart of the page. Prioritize scanability, price clarity, and immediate booking intent.
- Promotional messaging should be visible but not louder than the package decision itself.

### Proof and Service Area Links

- City pages and trust sections should feel useful and local, not SEO-spammy.
- Value props should be short, direct, and operational.

## Do

- Keep the tone confident, practical, and service-business simple.
- Optimize for first booking and repeat ease.
- Make mobile booking feel faster than calling around.

## Do Not

- Do not imitate luxury detailing sites with black, chrome, and overdesigned aggression.
- Do not hide pricing or package structure behind too much storytelling.
- Do not let motion overpower the booking funnel.
